## What Exactly is the Internet

Okay, now imagine a giant spider web stretching across the whole world, like the image I mentioned above, connecting millions of computers, phones, and servers. That's basically what the internet is. Instead of actual spider silk, these connections happen through cables, Wi-Fi signals, and even satellites.

Now think about how data from other countries reaches our computer. The other country could be anywhere, like from India to the USA, no matter how far away it is. As I mentioned before, these computer connections happen through cables. Yes, I mean actual cables! You might be amazed to learn that these cables are laid deep in the sea. They are very large and connect computers across the world.

![Undersea Cables](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1742509149741/5ade40fb-55b1-4496-b74d-7e9d904438ae.jpeg)

### A simpler way to think of it:

- The internet is like a huge city full of buildings (computers)
- Roads and highways would be (networks) that connect everything together
- Every building has an address (IP address) so that information **knows where to go**

Without these connections, you wouldn't be able to stream music, share memes, chat with friends, or even read this article.

## Step 1: Your Request is Sent

When you search for youtube.com, your computer first needs to find out where YouTube is on the internet. It's like wanting to visit a friend's house but not knowing the house number. You would ask a security guard or a neighbor for help. The security guard has a book with names and house numbers. On the internet, there's a service that helps your browser find YouTube's location. This is where the **DNS (Domain Name System)** comes in. It's like a security guard with a book that tells you YouTube is at this address: "142.250.182.206," which is the actual location of the website. See, I told you I would make this topic very easy.

---

## Step 2: Your Request Travels Across the Internet

Now that your request has an address, it starts its journey:

![Request Journey](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1742512876952/9d32e0b7-b49b-41a8-8fc2-3883fef039ba.png)

1. It **leaves your device** and goes to your Wi-Fi router
2. From the router, it travels through cables. Your **Internet Service Provider (ISP)** picks it up and sends it toward its destination
3. If the website's server is far away, your request might travel through **underground cables, satellites, or even undersea fiber-optic cables**
4. It finally reaches **YouTube's server**, which is basically a giant computer storing all the YouTube videos

---

## Step 3: The Server Sends Data Back

Once YouTube's server (essentially a giant computer storing all the data) receives your request, it quickly prepares the webpage and **sends the data back to you** following the same route but in reverse. Your browser then takes all that data and **displays the webpage on your screen**.

And guess what? All of this happens in just a fraction of a second! Isn't it fascinating how computers work?
